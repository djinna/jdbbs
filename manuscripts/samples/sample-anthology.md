# Khlongs, Subaks, Beaings

*Sam Chua*

In an IRL thread perhaps almost as strange as the stories in this volume, a motley crew of about two dozen researchers meet at a workshop about distributed AI and blockchain protocols in the outskirts of Bangkok. They visit and study regional traditional rice farming infrastructure ranging from canals to paddies to shrines and end up co-imagining a fictional world teeming with *artificial ghosts*, all in the course of a week.

The workshop was organized around three primary themes: the intersection of ancient agricultural practices with modern computational systems, the role of spirits and supernatural beliefs in Southeast Asian farming communities, and the potential for distributed autonomous organizations to manage shared water resources.

# Soda Sweet as Blood

*Spencer Nitkey*

My grandfather used to leave strawberry soda in the white and gold san chao thi spirit house on the corner of our property. He said the ghosts liked sweets and that the red reminded them of blood.

Every morning, before the sun had fully risen over the rice paddies, he would shuffle out in his worn sandals, a small bottle of Fanta clutched in his weathered hands. The spirit house stood on a single post, its miniature temple roof gleaming with fresh gold leaf that grandmother applied each new year.

"The phi need to eat too," he would say when I asked why we fed spirits that might not exist. "Better to be generous with those you cannot see."

# Terminal Dreams

*Anonymous*

The old terminal flickered to life:

```
>> INITIALIZATION SEQUENCE STARTED
>> CONNECTING TO GHOST NETWORK...
>> STATUS: 47 SPIRITS ONLINE
>> LATEST MESSAGE FROM NODE_PHI_023:
   "The water levels are low. Send offerings."
>> Communication line: open
```

Maria stared at the screen, her coffee growing cold beside her. The system had been dormant for years—ever since the last of the original programmers had passed away. Now it pulsed with an eerie green glow, cursor blinking expectantly.

She typed hesitantly:

```
>> Hello? Is anyone there?
>> RESPONSE FROM NODE_PHI_023:
   "We have always been here. Waiting."
>> Communication line: closed
```

*The Editors*

> "In nature nothing exists alone."

This collection emerged from a week of collaborative imagination in Thailand. The stories that follow are not quite fiction, not quite speculation—they exist in the liminal space between what is and what might be.

We thank our hosts, the spirits of the land, and the ghosts in our machines.
