# The Beginning

The year was 2087, and the world had changed in ways no one could have predicted. Sarah stood at the edge of the observation deck, watching the holographic advertisements dance across the night sky.

"Beautiful, isn't it?" The voice came from behind her. She didn't need to turn around to know who it was.

"Marcus." She kept her eyes on the lights. "I didn't expect to see you here."

The city below them hummed with the constant pulse of quantum processors, each one contributing to the vast network that kept civilization running. It was a marvel of engineering, a testament to human ingenuity.

---

After the break, she finally turned to face him. His face was older now, weathered by the years they'd spent apart.

"I came because of the message," he said. "The one you sent."

```
> INCOMING TRANSMISSION
> SOURCE: UNKNOWN
> PRIORITY: CRITICAL
> MESSAGE: "They know."
```

Sarah's heart raced. If Marcus had received it too, that meant the network was compromised.

# The Middle

They moved through the lower levels of the city, where the light didn't reach and the old ways still held sway. Here, people traded in whispers and secrets, their transactions invisible to the all-seeing eyes above.

*What we do in the shadows,*  
*echoes in the light above.*  
*The watchers cannot see*  
*what we've become.*

The poem had been their code, their way of communicating when all other channels failed.

"Do you remember," Marcus asked, "when we first wrote those words?"

"I remember everything," she replied.

# The End

In the end, it wasn't the technology that saved them. It wasn't the brilliant plans or the daring escapes. It was something far simpler: the connection between two people who refused to give up.

As the sun rose over the new world they'd helped create, Sarah allowed herself a small smile.

"Was it worth it?" Marcus asked.

She thought about all they'd sacrificed, all they'd gained.

"Yes," she said. "It was."

*The End*
